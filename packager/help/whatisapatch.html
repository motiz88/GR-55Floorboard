What is a Patch?

A “patch” is a unit of sound on the GR-55. In addition to settings determining the type of sound, a patch also contains effect settings. You can modify (edit) the settings of a patch, and save it as a “user patch.” (The factory supplied patches already built into the GR-55 are called “preset patches”).

Each patch contains all of the settings for the PCM 1 and 2 synthesizers, COSM Modeling instruments, Normal Pickup settings, and all of the effects for that memory location in the GR-55.

The GR-55 stores 297 (99x3) User patches, where you can assign each a number and a name, like USER : 20-2 : RnB Section and a myriad of settings, parameters and controls. It also contains permanent sound style patches, 90 (30x3) each for Lead, Rhythm and Other, that can be modified and save to user patches, but cannot be saved as Sound Styles. Modules and Effects Routing

I'll explain elsewhere how each of the tone and effects modules works. Here it is important to note that you can enable a tone (sound source) or an effects module by clicking on the Enable/Disable switches (rectangular buttons), which will light when enabled (as DELAY above) and be gray when disabled (as MFX above). You can also reroute the AMP(lifier), NS(noise suppression), and MOD(ulation) effects to come before or after the MFX(multi-effects) by clicking the STRUCTURE 1/2 buttons, where STRUCTURE 1 routes them after and STRUCTURE 2 routes the before. You can also bypass the MFX module and the AMP-NS-MOD modules entirely.
